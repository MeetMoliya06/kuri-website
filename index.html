✦ vs Google Reviews

Star ratings are broken.

As Kunal Shah noted — Indians rate 1 or 5. Nothing in between. When an average is just extremes averaged out, it tells you nothing about where to eat.

01

The Extremes Trap

Google ratings cluster at 1 and 5 — angry customers and die-hard fans dominate. The silent majority who had a decent meal? They never rate. The average of extremes tells you nothing.

02

1 in 10 Stars Is Fake

Google has the highest fake review rate of any platform — 10.7%. They removed 240 million policy-violating reviews in 2024 alone. You can't tell which ones you're trusting.

03

"Free Dessert for 5 Stars"

Restaurants openly bribe customers — free food, discounts, goodie bags — in exchange for 5-star ratings. The system is gamed. The score you see isn't earned.

→ how kuri fixes this

We don't ask you to pick a number. We ask "which did you enjoy more?" — binary comparisons that can't be faked, gamed, or pressured. Our ELO-based ranking only surfaces restaurants that consistently win head-to-head, from real diners, over time.

✦ How ranking works

Ranked by preference, not stars.

Instead of picking a number, you just tell us which restaurant you enjoyed more. Binary comparisons build rankings that are impossible to fake or game.
